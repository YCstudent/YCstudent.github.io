Report Writing Methods

Exam Sections for This Test

1. Introduction (Given)

  • Includes:
    1.1 Background
    1.2 Purpose of the report
    1.3 Method of the research
  • No need to write this section.

2. Findings

Problem-Solving Approach

  • Read the chart/table carefully to identify key information, such as categories, numbers, and trends.
  • Look for overall patterns (e.g., growth, decline, stability).
  • Compare different categories or time periods to identify significant differences or similarities.
  • Summarize the findings in a clear and logical order, starting with the most prominent feature.

Tips

  • Focus on key data points (e.g., highest/lowest values, significant changes).
  • Use objective and neutral language (e.g., avoid words like â€œinterestingâ€ or â€œsurprisingâ€).
  • Avoid including personal opinions or assumptions that are not supported by the data.

    • Highlight the most prominent trend, e.g., â€œFull-time students showed a steady increase from 1,000 in 2008 to 1,900 in 2014.â€

  3. Specific Data Points: Mention key values or percentages to support the trends identified, e.g., â€œPart-time students rose from 800 in 2008 to just under 1,200 in 2014.â€

3. Analytical Discussion

3.1 Interpretation of Data

Problem-Solving Approach:

  • Identify the general patterns in the data.
  • Analyze links between the two charts or figures.
  • Provide reasons for the observed general patterns using the links.
  • Reference the source of key data when explaining trends.

Tips:

  • Clearly identify cause-effect relationships or correlations.
  • Highlight how the two charts mutually reinforce conclusions to add depth to the interpretation.
  • Avoid personal assumptions and focus on evidence provided in the figures.

Template:

  1. General Pattern: Describe the overall trend, e.g., â€œThe findings show that studentsâ€™ homes were the most popular choice of location for doing research in 2014(Lee 2016, p. 12)â€
