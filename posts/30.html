Report Writing

Findings

Structure

Introduction

  • eg. The table compares the number of residents born in the UK, China, and India who live in Sydney and Melbourne.

Data Findings

  • eg. 224,685 people who live in Sydney were born in China, which was more than those born in the U.K. with 180,245 or India with 130,573. However, the largest number of foreign-born people from the three countries who live in Melbourne is from India with 161,078, followed by China with 155,998 and the U.K. with 133,300.

  • eg. The majority of people, making up 40% of the whole, getting news from the internet are between 19 and 40 years old. The next biggest group is the young people of 15 to 18 years old, with 35%. For the people between the ages 41 to 55, the percentage is 15%. The smallest group, 10%, is the people who are above 55 years old.

  • eg. Research results show that women marry for the first time later in life from below 20 years of age in 1965, to their early 30’s in 2015. However, the average age of divorce for women has significantly decreased over the same period, from their early 40’s to around 35 years of age, while the average age of women remarrying has remained reasonably steady, at around 44 years of age.

Conclusion

  • eg. Overall, more people who were born in the U.K., China, and India live in Sydney than in Melbourne.

  • eg. Based on the above, the majority of people getting news from the internet are between 15 to 40 years old, making up 75%.

In-text referencing

  • eg. Irvine 2018, p.41

Step 1

  • Identify the purpose and the subject of charts to choose the appropriate language expressions.

Step 2

  • Organise data to structure the presentation clearly and logically: from the highest to lowest; similarities and differences.

Interpretation of data

Structure

  • Summarize the Link (Chart1 <–> Chart2)

  • Reason from other factors (Chart1)

  • Other factors (Chart2) / Contrast (Chart1)

  • Conclusion (Chart2 <—> Chart1)

Details

Sentence 1

  • This sentence links the fact that marriage is not seen to be as important as other factors for fulfilling life to the fact that women are getting married for the first time at a later age. This could mean that women in 2015 do not see marriage as being important as in 1965.

  • eg. The findings show that women in Hume do not consider marriage as important as many other factors for having a fulfilling life, and are getting married at a later age than women in 1965.

Sentence 2

  • Here, we look at what the other factors are in more detail and point out that many of them are related to personal (friendship & goals) and professional (job & education) development. This shows the factors that women find more important than marriage.

  • eg. Personal and professional development such as education, career and achieving personal goals are all considered to be more important than marriage.

Sentence 3

  • We’ve already noted that women in Hume were older in 2015 when they married for the first time, but the other main trend is that they are getting divorced younger, so marriages don’t seem to last as long in 2015 as they did in 1965. This seems strange as women listed friendship and relationships as the most important factor to fulfilment, so this link was also noted.

  • eg. The average age of divorce for women becomes younger, meaning that marriages for women in Hume have lasted for shorter periods of time despite relationships being rated as part of the most important factor for having a fulfilling life.

Sentence 4

  • As a concluding sentence, the main point that we can take from the findings is that marriage patterns have changed (women are getting married later and divorced earlier), and that this may be related to marriage not being as important to women as other factors.

  • eg. Based on the above, the findings indicate a significant shift in patterns of marriage as women in Hume prioritise other aspects of life as being more important for fulfillment.

Evaluation of data

Structure

Relevance to purpose (Why you selected the data)
Result (base on findings/purpose)

  • Chart 2 —> Purpose 2
  • Chart 2 —> Purpose 1
Solution to the issue (base on findings/purpose)

Example

  • The important findings regarding changes in the marital status of women may have some impact on Hume society.

  • The change in age of women’s first marriage and divorce indicate that women are spending more of their life unmarried in 2015 than in 1965.

  • This may result in an increase in the number of single parent families or young families with older parents than in the past.

  • Women in Hume see both relationships and having children as more important than getting married, so this may lead to an increase in the number of families with a single parent or step-mother or father.

  • As a result of these trends, social services in Hume may need to focus on a much broader definition of the family than may have been expected in 1965.

  • The prioritisation of successful career and higher education by many women suggests that issues might need to be considered in order to address any impact of changes to marriage patterns on society and support the women of Hume.

Process

Limitations of Research Findings

  • There are two factors which limit the research.

  • Firstly, only secondary sources about the marital status of a number of women are collected.

  • No details were given showing how the data from Young’s research was collected.

  • As a result, the data in the report may not provide a complete understanding of the issues of women’s marriage and employment in Hume.

  • Secondly, another limitation in the research is the number of sources collected.

  • The analysis of the research findings is based on only one report, which does not cover enough detail to help explain why the changes in female marital status have taken place.

Recommendations for the Future Research

  • Further research is recommended in order to collect primary sources about female marital status, thus providing more valid data to compare with actual changes in current Hume society.

  • In addition, there should be more sources selected with different perspectives on the changes in female marital and employment status, so as to give a more detailed understanding of the issues involved.
